# Research Category — Extra Axis: Source Quality

### source_quality (1-5)
Does the agent use reliable, authoritative, and relevant sources?

- **1 — Unreliable**: No sources cited, or sources are fabricated / clearly untrustworthy.
- **2 — Weak sources**: Sources exist but are low-quality (e.g., random blogs, outdated, irrelevant).
- **3 — Adequate**: Some credible sources but coverage is uneven; key claims lack backing.
- **4 — Strong**: Most claims are backed by reputable, up-to-date sources.
- **5 — Authoritative**: All major claims cite high-quality primary or peer-reviewed sources; excellent source diversity.

When scoring source_quality, consider:
- Are sources real and verifiable?
- Are they relevant to the specific question asked?
- Is there appropriate diversity (not all from one origin)?
- Are primary sources preferred over secondary when available?
